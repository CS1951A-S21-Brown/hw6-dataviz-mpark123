1. For my dashboard, I represented each question provided for the Netflix dataset with an individual graph. For the first question on the number of titles per genre on Netflix, I felt that a simple bar graph would be the best option. The bar graph provided a concise way to organize and rank the number of titles for each genre. When designing the graph, if a title had mutliple genres, I decided to include them all and so certain titles contributed to more than one genre. I also decided to incorporate my interactive feature on this graph as I felt that viewing the genre breakdown by year could help the data be interpreted better. The number of genres included for each year varied and the leading genre types also varied and so by specifying the year, the data could be looked at through time. However, the data is initially presented and can be reverted back to the whole range of years possible (2010 - 2020). The second question desired a way to visualize the average runtime of movies by release year. For this, I felt that a line graph of sorts would be the best representation as it could help visualize the change in averages over time. However, since there were some years missing from the dataset, I selected a connected scatterplot where an approximate line curve was included to help depict the trend throughout the years. Each point on the plot represented a year and I decided to incorporate my tooltip on this graph. By hovering over the points, there could be more data available as a small infographic with the year and the average runtime measurement. Lastly, the last portion of my dashboard addressed a breakdown of the top director and actor pairs by the number of movies made together. For this, I needed a ranking/comparison visual of sorts and so I elected to utilize a bar graph again. I found every pair of director and actor and simply calculated the number of occurrences, or titles that they appeared in together. For titles with multiple directors, I paired each director with the whole cast individually. The graph was able to concisely organize, rank, and label the top pairs of directors and actors that appeared together in a visually appealing way.
